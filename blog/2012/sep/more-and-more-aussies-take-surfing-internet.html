More and more Aussies take up surfing... the Internet

Magda12 September 2012

This month, the Digital Futures 2012 Report on the Internet in Australia was issued by the ARC Centre of Excellence for Creative Industries and Innovation.  Based on a survey taken in 2011, it follows trends in Internet usage across the country, and attempts to point out major opportunities and potential issues presented as more Australians get online.

Cross(Functional) had a look through the report, and here's some of what we found:

General usage

  • 86.8% of Australians said they use the Internet, which is up by 6% compared to last year.
  • 96.3% of Australian households have broadband access.
  • An increasing number of households use VOIP services, whether it is provided by their ISP, or by services like Skype.
  • The number of households accessing the Internet via a mobile phone or mobile device has increased from 15% to 37% over the past two years.
  • 9 out of 10 Australians describe the internet as being either 'important' or 'very important' part of their lives.

As you can see, people are still getting online in droves. An increase of 6% may not seem like much until you realise that's over a million people in just one year. Furthermore, the massive increase in mobile Internet usage in the past two years alone is a movement of deep significance, especially for web developers and business owners.

However, it's not all surf and sunshine; there is still a distinct divide amongst socioeconomic groups when it comes to internet usage. If you are a student, young person, earn a high income or are highly educated, then you are more likely to access the net than if you are retired, a homemaker, less educated or of low income.

Affordability seems to be the reason why large number of low income users still do not have access to broadband at home (the figure currently stands at 4 out of 10). However, the gap in the 'digital divide' has decreased since the Digital Futures report was first conducted in 2007.

The study has found that Internet usage amongst Australians continues to grow in three main aspects:

  1. The number of users getting online
  2. The number of activities being conducted online
  3. The frequency of activities conducted online

The report has also found a rapid growth in the following specific Internet applications:

Online Communications

  • 68% of Australians see the internet as a medium that increases people's contact with friends, and 62% believe it increases contact with family.
  • The usage of social media has shown a slight decrease compared to the last reports conducted in 2007 and 2009.
  • The usage of VOIP to make telephone calls is continuing to grow, from 30% in 2009 to 39% in 2011.

Accessing Media

  • The Internet has become the most important source of news and information for Australians, and its importance has shown a slight increase in the past two years.
  • Just over 75% of users said that the internet was either an 'important  or 'very important' source of news and information.
  • 7 out of 10 users will go online for international or local breaking news.
  • Fewer users are watching TV, because they can download movies from the net. The number of movie downloaders has grown from 10% to 20% of users in the last 3 years.
  • When it comes to paid media, 70% of users said they would not pay for an online newspaper, and only 8% would be willing to pay the same amount for the online edition as they would for a printed copy.

Source of Entertainment

  • The Internet is becoming a 'very important' source of entertainment for Australians.
  • 30% of users are buying music online. This figure has doubled since 2009.
  • Searching the the net for information about restaurants, food, and recipes is continuing to grow amongst Internet users.
  • 1 out of 10 Internet users use it for gambling. In 2007, this figure was 1 in 20.

Shopping

  • In comparison to other countries, Australians are the most frequent shoppers online.
  • There is a strong preference from Australian Internet users to buy from Australian based sites.
  • Online security, limitations of products, and quality are some of the concerns expressed by users who shop online.

The report also mentions the effect the Internet has on politics. The majority of internet users and non-internet users believe the internet has become an important medium for political campaigning, but they're not yet certain about whether it would help them have their say in government policy and affairs.

When it comes to the regulation of the internet, the majority of Internet users believe the internet is not over regulated. 84% believe there should be some restrictions on internet access for children, but most believe that this would be best shared between families, schools, governments and ISPs. Also, when it came to the prospect of the government checking up on Australians' online activities, 28% of respondents expressed concern.

The full report can be downloaded from: http://www.cci.edu.au/projects/digital-futures
